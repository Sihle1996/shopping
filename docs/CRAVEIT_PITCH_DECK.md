## Slide 2 — The Problem

**Ordering food in South Africa is broken for everyone involved.**

**For customers:**
- No single platform that serves township restaurants, home cooks, and formal restaurants equally
- Generic apps with no personalisation — you scroll endlessly with no guidance
- No way to order as a group without complicated splits

**For restaurants:**
- Expensive commission-only platforms take 25–30% per order
- No tools to manage inventory, promotions, or driver logistics in one place
- Informal and unregistered businesses are locked out entirely

**For delivery platforms:**
- Fraud from unverified merchants and drivers
- No intelligence — orders, drivers, and inventory managed manually

---

## Slide 3 — The Solution

**CraveIt is a multi-tenant food delivery platform built for the South African market.**

One platform. Three experiences.

- **Customers** — browse menus, order, track deliveries, earn loyalty points
- **Restaurants** — manage their menu, orders, drivers, promotions, and analytics from one dashboard
- **Drivers** — accept orders, navigate deliveries, verify with OTP, track earnings

And unlike global platforms, CraveIt works for **both formal businesses and informal sellers** — from registered restaurants to home cooks and spaza shops.

## Slide 5 — AI-Powered Intelligence

**CraveIt is the first South African delivery platform with AI built into the restaurant dashboard.**

**For restaurant owners:**
- Flags slow-moving menu items: *"Veggie Wrap hasn't sold in 14 days — consider a discount"*
- Predicts stock shortages: *"Smash Burger will hit low stock in ~3 days at current rate"*
- Writes menu descriptions from just the item name
- Generates promotion suggestions based on 30-day sales data
- Summarises customer reviews into sentiment scores and action points
- Analyses support tickets and drafts replies automatically

**For operations:**
- Flags suspicious or unusual orders in real time
- Suggests the best available driver for each order based on location and load
- Forecasts high-demand periods: *"Expect high demand Friday 6–9pm — add a driver"*
- Tracks driver performance and flags consistently late deliveries

**Powered by Claude AI (Anthropic) — the same AI behind Claude.ai**

## Slide 8 — Business Model

**Multiple revenue streams. Low risk.**

| Stream | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Commission** | % per order processed through the platform |
| **Subscription** | Monthly fee for premium restaurant features (analytics, AI, unlimited items) |
| **Verification fee** | Small fee charged to stores on onboarding (covers VerifyNow cost + margin) |
| **Featured placement** | Restaurants pay to be promoted at the top of the app |
| **White-label licensing** | Sell the platform to other delivery operators who want their own branded version |

**Unit economics per restaurant:**
- Onboarding verification: ~R9 cost, charged at R25–50
- Monthly subscription: R299–R999/month depending on tier
- Commission: negotiated per restaurant size

## Slide 11 — Go-To-Market Strategy

**Start local. Scale nationally.**

**Phase 1 — Pilot (Month 1–3)**
- Onboard 10–20 restaurants in one area (Johannesburg South)
- Mix of formal and informal sellers
- Use feedback to refine onboarding and delivery experience

**Phase 2 — City rollout (Month 3–6)**
- Expand across Johannesburg, then Pretoria and Cape Town
- Targeted social media campaigns (township-focused)
- Partnership with food business associations and spaza networks

**Phase 3 — National scale (Month 6–12)**
- All major cities
- Enterprise restaurant chains
- White-label licensing to regional operators

**Customer acquisition:**
- Restaurants: direct outreach + LinkedIn + food business WhatsApp groups
- Customers: social media + word of mouth + restaurant referrals
- Drivers: Gumtree, Facebook groups, direct recruitment
